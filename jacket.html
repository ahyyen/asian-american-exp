A Jacket
Tossed On
The Bed

"If you go outside and there is wind, put on your jacket or else you'll get sick." This knowledge has been imparted onto me by my Asian family since childhood. Though a simple Google search proves otherwise; while extreme cold can cause hypothermia, you won't catch a cold or flu from a light autumn breeze.

I'm not sure why it felt like my parents were always trying to overdress me. Maybe it has something to do with the climate being warmer where they're from. Maybe they just wanted to be prepared, to be safe rather than sorry. Or maybe it's just because of the myth that colds are literally caused by cold. As kids, my brother and I may have found the constant nagging annoying, but in the end it's just our parents trying their best to look out for us and keep us healthy.
